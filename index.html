Update: How ourBRIDGE is Responding to Border Patrol & ICE Presence and Ways to Support Families and the Charlotte Community Today
 

Dear *{{First Name}}*, 

News of Border Patrol in Charlotte has left many in our community afraid and uncertain about the safety of their families and loved ones. We want to remind all of our Charlotte neighbors that you belong here. This city is our home.

We believe that no child should have to worry about being separated from their family.

We’re grateful for the outpouring of support and ask for your patience as we put systems in place – both to make it easier for community members to help and to protect the safety and trust of our neighbors. This Community Advocate email list is where you’ll receive our most detailed updates.

ourBRIDGE Immediate Response to ICE/DHS/CBP Presence in Charlotte:

1. Adjust afterschool routines as needed while keeping 400+ families informed with clear, verifiable updates.

2. Provide individualized, multilingual support and expand access to mental health care through our play therapist and partner counselors

3. Distribute emergency funds and assist neighbors with rides, groceries, and resources.

4. Host multilingual Know Your Rights and preparedness workshops and offer Trauma-Informed Care training for organizations serving children affected by ICE/DHS/CBP activity.

Below you'll find ways you can specifically support ourBRIDGE as well as the wider Charlotte community.

Thank you for your continued care and support, 

Sil Ganzó

Executive Director & Founder
sil@joinourbridge.org

4. Join us on Wednesday the 19th at 5:30 PM at our East site to hear directly from our team. This event was previously scheduled, and we’ll now include updates on what’s happening and how you can get involved. RSVP HERE.

5. Donate to support our crisis response efforts. While we work hard to maximize resources, our federal funds remain at risk. To respond without relying on reserve funds, we need additional unrestricted support. Please DONATE HERE.

 

 

Ways you can support Charlotte Neighbors

1. Charlotte East 2nd Community Response Call: Hosted by Charlotte EAST on Sunday, November 16th at 5 PM via Zoom. The link will be shared on their social media and by email. *Interpretation will be available in Spanish, French, and Swahili.

2. ICE Watch Training: Monday, November 17th @6pm Sign up here

3. Report ICE Activity: Call the Comunidad Colectiva hotline at 704-740-7737 and share: Date and time, location (including intersections, city, and state), clear photos and videos. *Please share this number with family and friends.

4. Follow Carolina Migrant Network and Comunidad Colectiva to receive ICE presence alerts. Text "Join" or "Juntos" to 980-336-4996 to sign up. Consider joining their Rapid Response Team, allowing them to verify ICE presence quickly 

5. Follow Julian Berger @WFAE for local immigration news in Charlotte.

6. Support local businesses - Spend money at immigrant-owned businesses in South and East Charlotte, especially those likely to be affected by heavy ICE presence. Charlotte East compiled a list of east businesses here. 

7. Print and share “Know Your Rights” cards: Available here.

8. Create secure group chats for close neighbors: Build group chats for your block or building to stay connected and share updates.
